Why Action Matters

Racism is a systemic problem, but individuals and communities can help create change. Taking action can reduce inequalities in education, housing, jobs, and justice, and help build a fairer society.

Practical Steps You Can Take

  • Educate Yourself: Read books, watch documentaries, and learn about systemic racism like The Other Wes Moore.
  • Support School Clubs: Volunteer, donate, or promote school clubs fighting racial inequality.
  • Advocate for Fair Policies: Contact local representatives, attend town halls, and support legislation that addresses inequity in schools, policing, and housing.
  • Challenge Racism in Your Community: Speak up against discrimination and tell your peers about it.
  • Share Knowledge: Use social media, blogs, or IA projects to raise awareness and educate others about systemic racism.
